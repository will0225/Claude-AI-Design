Findings, Exposure & Recommendations
CRITICAL
F-01
Chronic unit kept in service while spend accumulated (Suite 4600-120, AC-6)
Five service visits in 19 days and $2,444 of May repairs preceded the replacement decision. A medical tenant (Choice Parenteral Nutrition) carried comfort risk throughout; as of 6/15 the approved replacement is still pending a curb adaptor.

ACTION › Expedite install — obtain firm date and curb-adaptor ETA from Solace; confirm heat-pump model and SMUD rebate registration before work proceeds.
HIGH
F-02
Q1 PM deficiencies flagged "estimate to follow" — no matching estimate or repair found in the GL
The 2/16 inspection identified pitted contactors, scraping/seized inducer bearings, a non-opening gas valve and a buzzing transformer across several suites. No corresponding proposal or R&M invoice for those specific items appears in the period ledger; some later no-heat / no-cool emergencies trace to the same equipment.

ACTION › Pull the Solace Q1 estimate, bid-level it, and close or formally defer each line — quantify the deferred-maintenance backlog in dollars.
HIGH
F-03
Chase-the-failure pattern on Suite 4600-100
$2,539 of heating repairs in Dec 2025 (intermittent heat, thermostat, roll-out & high-limit switches) preceded a $5,500 compressor replacement on the same unit — money spent repairing an asset already near end-of-life.

ACTION › Apply a repair-vs-replace threshold (e.g. 40% of replacement cost) before authorizing further T&M on units of this vintage.
MEDIUM
F-04
No evident warranty register — risk of paying twice
The 4600-220 blower motor carries a 1-year parts & labor warranty (exp. ~6/3/27) and the Solace contract carries a 1-year workmanship guarantee on all repairs. Without a tracked register, warrantied failures may be re-billed as new T&M.

ACTION › Stand up a warranty register (part, labor, equipment) cross-referenced to invoice numbers; flag any repeat failure inside a warranty window.
MEDIUM
F-05
Q2 PM documentation thinner than contracted Schedule B scope
The 5/8 work order reads "check, clean condenser coils, swap filters." The contracted quarterly checklist contains 14+ inspection points per unit type plus logged readings; the narrative does not evidence full execution.

ACTION › Require per-unit checklist completion and amp/voltage readings on each quarterly visit, attached to the invoice, per Exhibit A.
